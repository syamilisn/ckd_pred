Terms and conditions* Usually, CKD does not give any signs until the kidney is damaged beyond repair. The disease is increasing rapidly as per the studies which conclude that the hospitalization cases due to CKD have increased by 6.23 percent per year. And the global mortality rate remains fixed. There are few diagnostic tests to check the condition of CKD such as: 1) eGFR test: It stands for estimated Glomerular Filtration Rate (eGFR), which analyses the rate of filtration by glomeruli, present in the kidneys. 2) Urine test: For detecting anomalies in urine that point to defective kidney functions. 3) Blood tests: For detecting the presence of serum creatinine or urea in blood.
